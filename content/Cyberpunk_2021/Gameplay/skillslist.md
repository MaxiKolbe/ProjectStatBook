> [!info] Skill Levels
> Skills range from **1–10**.
>
> **1–3** — Book-knowledge beginner to competent amateur  
> **4–5** — Competent professional  
> **6–7** — Skilled professional  
> **8–9** — World-class expert  
> **10** — Peak human ability

The following scale provides a **general interpretation** of what each skill level represents in the world.

---

## Skill Level Benchmarks

### Level 0 — Untrained
No formal training.

The character may attempt basic tasks but lacks proper technique.

Examples:
- Civilian firing a gun for the first time
- Someone attempting repairs without knowledge

---

### Level 1 — Basic Familiarity
Minimal exposure to the skill.

Represents:
- hobbyists
- beginners
- early learners

Examples:
- someone who took an introductory class
- someone learning guitar
- someone casually practicing a sport

---

### Level 2 — Novice
Some training but inconsistent ability.

Represents:
- amateurs
- recreational skill level

Examples:
- recreational athlete
- casual shooter
- someone who can perform simple repairs

---

### Level 3 — Competent Amateur
Reliable amateur capability.

Represents:
- experienced hobbyists
- entry-level practical ability

Examples:
- high school athlete
- amateur musician
- someone comfortable using basic tools

---

### Level 4 — Trained Professional
Formal training and professional competence.

Represents:
- police officers
- trained technicians
- entry-level professionals

Examples:
- police firearm training
- licensed mechanic
- working musician

---

### Level 5 — Experienced Professional
Seasoned professionals with practical experience.

Represents:
- skilled workers
- veteran professionals
- individuals trusted with responsibility

Examples:
- experienced police officer
- trained infantry soldiers
- senior engineer
- established performer

---

### Level 6 — Advanced Professional
Highly trained specialists who exceed normal professional standards.

Represents:
- elite military training
- highly respected professionals
- individuals with extensive field experience

Examples:
- military veteran
- advanced tactical operators
- nationally competitive athletes
- expert technicians

---

### Level 7 — Elite Specialist
Among the most capable individuals within a country or region.

Represents:
- rare talent even among professionals
- individuals with exceptional training and ability

Examples:
- top tactical operatives
- elite mercenaries
- nationally renowned performers
- master-level fighters

---

### Level 8 — World-Class Expert
Internationally recognized mastery.

Represents:
- the best in the world
- individuals whose abilities stand out globally

Examples:
- Olympic medalists
- world-famous martial artists
- legendary performers
- elite global operators

---

### Level 9 — Legendary
Extremely rare skill level.

Represents:
- global icons
- once-in-a-generation talent
- individuals whose abilities are widely known and feared

Examples:
- historical champions
- legendary gunslingers
- revolutionary innovators
- once-in-a-generation talent

---

### Level 10 — Peak Human Ability
The absolute limit of human capability.

Represents:
- mythical mastery
- individuals who define the pinnacle of their field

Examples:
- martial arts grandmasters
- genius inventors
- legendary gunslingers
- historically unmatched experts

# Special Abilities (Role Skills)

These skills are tied to specific character **roles/careers**.

### Authority (Cop)
Ability to leverage legal authority, intimidate criminals, and command compliance.

### Charismatic Leadership (Rockerboy)
Influence crowds, inspire followers, and mobilize people emotionally.

### Combat Sense (Solo)
Enhanced battlefield awareness, danger perception, and reaction timing in combat.

### Credibility (Media)
The ability to gather information and convince the public of its truth.

### Family (Nomad)
Access to nomad family networks, resources, vehicles, and support.

### Interface (Netrunner)
Ability to connect to and operate within cyberspace networks.

### Jury Rig (Techie)
Improvisational ability to repair or modify equipment quickly.

### Medical Tech (Medtech)
Advanced medical knowledge for diagnosis, treatment, and surgery.

### Resources (Corporate)
Access to corporate funding, influence, and organizational resources.

### Streetdeal (Fixer)
Finding illegal goods, arranging deals, and negotiating street-level transactions.
