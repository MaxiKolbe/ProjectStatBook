title: Cyberpunk Mechanics
---

The character statistics and skills used in the Cyberpunk 2021 archive are inspired by the **Cyberpunk 2020 tabletop roleplaying system**.

These mechanics are used **loosely for narrative reference** rather than strict gameplay, but the underlying framework remains similar to the original game.

---

# Core Concept

Characters attempt actions using a **Skill Check**.

The basic formula is:
1d10 + STAT + SKILL

→ [Skills List](./skillslist)

---

# Task Difficulties

Every action has a **Difficulty Value (DV)** determined by the referee or narrator.

| Difficulty | DV |
|---|---|
| Easy | 10+ |
| Average | 15+ |
| Difficult | 20+ |
| Very Difficult | 25+ |
| Nearly Impossible | 30+ |

When attempting an action:

1d10 + Stat + Skill

If the total **meets or exceeds the Difficulty**, the action succeeds.

If the total is **lower**, the action fails.

---

# Giving It Your Best Shot

When making a Skill Check, first determine **which Stat is most appropriate** for the action.

Examples:

- **REFLEXES** for physical balance, reaction, or combat
- **INTELLIGENCE** for puzzles, codebreaking, or technical problems
- **TECHNOLOGY** for repairing or manipulating devices
- **COOL / COMPOSURE** for intimidation or keeping calm under pressure

Then choose **one relevant Skill**.

Example:

A character trying to pick a lock might use:

1d10 + TECH + Pick Lock

---

# Opposed Tasks

Sometimes characters directly compete against each other.

In these cases:

Both characters roll

1d10 + Stat + Skill

The **higher result wins**.

If the results tie, the **defender wins**.

---

# Difficulty Modifiers

Situational factors may **increase or decrease difficulty**.

Examples include:

- Poor lighting
- Stressful environments
- Injury
- Advanced equipment
- Assistance from allies

Multiple modifiers may stack depending on circumstances.

---

# Critical Success & Failure

### Critical Success

If a player rolls a **natural 10**, they score a **critical success**.

Roll another **1d10 and add it to the total**.

This represents exceptional luck or performance.

---

### Automatic Failure

If a player rolls a **natural 1**, the action **automatically fails**.

Roll another **1d10** and consult the **Fumble Table**.

---

# Fumble Examples

Fumbles represent things going wrong.

Examples include:

### Reflex (Combat)

- Weapon jams
- You drop your weapon
- You accidentally hit yourself
- You hit an ally

### Reflex (Athletics)

- Minor failure
- Stumble or fall
- Muscle strain or injury

### Tech

- Device breaks
- Tool slips
- Repair attempt damages the equipment

### Empathy (Social)

- You offend someone
- Your attempt backfires socially
- You escalate a situation unintentionally

### Intelligence

- You misunderstand the situation
- You misread important information
- You completely miss an obvious clue

# Stats

> [!info] Stat System
> **Stats range from 1–10**
>
> • 1 — extremely poor  
> • 5 — average human  
> • 10 — exceptional / gifted

Stats represent **natural talent and physical capability**.

### BODY

Physical strength, durability, and toughness.

Affects:

- melee combat
- endurance
- resisting damage
- physical feats

---

### REFLEXES

Speed, reaction time, coordination.

Affects:

- gun combat
- dodging attacks
- athletics
- driving

---

### TECHNOLOGY

Understanding and manipulating technology.

Affects:

- repairs
- engineering
- hacking equipment
- modifying gear

---

### INTELLIGENCE

Reasoning, analysis, and problem solving.

Affects:

- deduction
- knowledge
- technical understanding
- investigation

---

### COMPOSURE (COOL)

Mental toughness and emotional control.

Affects:

- intimidation
- resisting fear
- maintaining focus in stressful situations

---

### ATTRACTIVENESS

Physical appearance and charm.

Affects:

- social impressions
- seduction
- persuasion

---

### EMPATHY

Emotional awareness and ability to understand people.

Affects:

- social skills
- persuasion
- reading others

> [!warning] Humanity
> Humanity is dependent on **Empathy**.
>
> When Humanity reaches **0**, the person enters **cyberpsychosis**.

Cyberware installation reduces humanity.

---

### LUCK

Represents fortune and timing.

Luck points may be spent to **add to rolls before they occur**.

# Skills

> [!info] Skill Levels
> Skills range from **1–10**.
>
> **1–3** — Book-knowledge beginner to competent amateur  
> **4–5** — Competent professional  
> **6–7** — Skilled professional  
> **8–9** — World-class expert  
> **10** — Peak human ability

Skills represent **training and learned ability**, layered on top of stats.

# Improving Skills

Characters improve their abilities by earning **Improvement Points (IP)**.

IP represent learning through **practice, study, and real-world experience**.

To raise a skill:

Current Skill Level × 10 = IP required

Example:

- Raising a skill from **4 → 5** costs **40 IP**
- Raising **5 → 6** costs **50 IP**

---

## IP Multipliers

Not all skills are equally easy to learn.

Some skills have an **IP Multiplier**, which increases the number of Improvement Points required to raise the skill.

The multiplier represents skills that are:

- harder to master
- more technical
- specialized training

To calculate the cost:
(Current Skill Level × 10) × IP Multiplier

Example:
Choi Li Fut has an **IP multiplier of 3**.
To raise the skill from **4 → 5**:
(4 × 10) × 3 = **120 IP**
instead of **40 IP**.

---

# Ways to Gain Improvement Points

### Study & Practice

Self-teaching through study and repetition.

---

### Being Taught

Learning from someone with a **higher skill level**.

---

### Direct Experience

The most effective way to gain IP.

When characters successfully use skills during missions or adventures, they may gain improvement points.

The referee awards IP based on how meaningful the skill use was.

---

## Example IP Awards

| IP Award | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Skill used in the area |
| 2 | Skill used effectively |
| 3 | Frequent and effective use |
| 4 | Creative or clever use |
| 5 | Very clever use |
| 6 | Extremely effective |
| 7 | Critical to the mission |
| 8 | Critical for the whole team |
| 9 | Incredible achievement |

---

# Reputation

Characters develop **Reputation** based on their actions.
Reputation influences how others react.

Example levels:

| Reputation | Recognition |
|---|---|
| 1 | Known by witnesses |
| 3 | Known locally |
| 5 | Recognized across districts |
| 7 | Famous across the city |
| 10 | Known worldwide |

---

# Faceoffs

When two characters attempt to intimidate or dominate each other socially:
1d10 + COOL + Reputation

The higher result gains psychological advantage.
The loser may hesitate or back down.

---

This system serves as a **reference framework for Cyberpunk 2021 characters**, helping describe capability, training, and reputation in the story world.
